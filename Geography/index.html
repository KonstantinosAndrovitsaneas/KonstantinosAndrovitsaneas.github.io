WHAT ARE EARTHQUAKES AND HOW DO THEY WORK?

The Earth is made of Tectonic Plates, they are always moving. However these plates carry the posibility of comming in contact with each other. When they do, they create the phenomenon of earthquakes. There are three ways tectonic plates can come in contact and whenever they do, they release energy waves that run through earth's crust, creating a shaky motion.

FUN FACT:

The magnitude of earthquakes is measured by a logarithmic system called: Richter Scale.
The intensity is measured on the Mercalli scale.

WHERE ARE EARTHQUAKES HAPPENING IN CANADA?

Earthquakes are most common around tectonic plate boundries.(Where tectonic plates collide) In Canada there is only one province near a tectonic plate, to feel the effect. That is the coast of British Collumbia.

Enviornmental

Earthquakes can cause damage to the environment and wildlife by disrupting the ecosystems and releasing toxic pollutants. Also they can cause Tsunamis and landslides which both cause massive damage to the environment and human societies.

Political

The government in turn can play two parts, that can have both a positive and negative effect. The government can regulate the safety of structures to reduce the effect of the earthquakes, at the cost of higher prices and tax money to pay inspectors. As well as assisting with damage cost which also increases taxes.

How would earthquakes affect change the land over time?

Earthquakes can impact human societies and physical landscapes in many ways. From changing society practices or society locations to changing communities over time. Over time as human technology advances areas with large earthquakes will begin to have larger buildings and communities. Japan is a great example of a great engineering feet!

Economic

Earthquakes can and will cause damage to man made structures such as homes, buildings and other structures. This in turn causes taxes to increase. Also this results in tax money being removed from other areas such as school and extra-curricular activities to fund the damage cost of the earthquakes.

Social

Earthquakes cause a lot of damage. Therefore can cause buildings to be demolished and other structures. This causes communities to be split apart because of relocation. Also mental health is impacted due to this surprise experience. Having side effects such as:

  • Anxiety and fear
  • Displacement and homelessness
  • Economic losses and financial stress
  • Psychological trauma and post-traumatic stress disorder
